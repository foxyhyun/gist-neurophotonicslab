Neuropathic Pain

DRG Neuroimmune Translation

Our pain research focuses on how peripheral sensory systems are organized and how nerve injury remodels these circuits to drive neuropathic pain. We focus on the dorsal root ganglion (DRG) as a key site where neurons, glia, immune cells, and vasculature interact to shape sensory transduction and transmission. Using mouse models of acute and chronic pain, we combine molecular, cellular, and behavioral approaches to identify pathway- and modality-specific mechanisms and to inform more precise therapeutic strategies for neuropathic pain.

AI-based Neuro Electrophysiology Platform

HD-MEA CSD AI analytics

We develop an AI-driven electrophysiology platform that integrates high-density microelectrode array (HD-MEA) recordings with advanced signal processing and machine learning to quantify neural population dynamics, map network reorganization, and identify disease-relevant electrophysiological biomarkers.

  • Spike/LFP feature extraction and event detection at scale
  • CSD-based spatiotemporal dynamics and propagation analysis
  • Representation learning for phenotype discovery and classification
